## 1. Glossary & Key Terminologies

* **UHI (Urban Heat Island):** An urban area that is significantly warmer than its surrounding rural areas due to human activities, dark surfaces (asphalt), and lack of vegetation.
* **LST (Land Surface Temperature):** The radiative skin temperature of the land surface, measured from space in Kelvin and converted to Celsius.
* **NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index):** A measure of live green vegetation. Ranges from -1 to +1. High values (>0.4) indicate dense vegetation (trees, crops). Low values indicate built-up concrete or barren land.
* **Albedo ($\alpha$):** The measure of the diffuse reflection of solar radiation out of the total solar radiation. Ranges from 0 (perfect absorber, e.g., black asphalt) to 1 (perfect reflector, e.g., fresh snow or white roofs).
* **AHE (Anthropogenic Heat Emissions):** Heat generated by human activities. Divided into:
  * **BAH (Building AHE):** Heat released by HVAC systems and building infrastructure.
  * **TAH (Transportation AHE):** Heat from vehicle exhausts, friction, and dark road absorption.
  * **IAH (Industrial AHE):** Heat from factories and heavy manufacturing.
  * **MAH (Metabolic AHE):** Body heat released by densely packed human populations.
* **RHII (Relative Heat Island Intensity):** A metric describing how much hotter a specific urban pixel is compared to the surrounding rural/cropland average.
* **LCZ (Local Climate Zone):** A classification system for urban landscapes based on their thermal properties (e.g., dense trees, open low-rise, compact high-rise).
* **PINN (Physics-Informed Neural Network):** A neural network whose loss function is penalized if it breaks known laws of physics (in this case, thermodynamics).
* **NSGA-II:** Non-dominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II. An evolutionary algorithm used to optimize multiple conflicting objectives (e.g., maximizing cooling while minimizing the intervention budget).

## 2. The Datasets & Attributes

### 2.1 Raw Data (`data/raw/`)
Extracted directly from Google Earth Engine (GEE) at a 30-meter resolution across the 1,500 sq km Delhi-NCR bounding box.
* `LST_Kelvin.tif`: Landsat 8 Collection 2 Level 2 Surface Temperature. Single band.
* `Sentinel2_Features.tif`: Sentinel-2 Harmonized Surface Reflectance. Contains two engineered bands: Band 1 (NDVI) and Band 2 (Albedo).

### 2.2 Processed Tabular Data (`data/processed/delhi_thermal_features.csv`)
The 3D rasters are flattened into a 1.5 million row CSV. Each row represents a single 30m x 30m pixel.
* **LST_Kelvin:** Raw temperature in Kelvin.
* **NDVI:** Vegetation proxy (0 to 1 scale roughly in land).
* **Albedo:** Surface reflectivity proxy.
* **LST_Celsius:** The target variable ($T_C = T_K - 273.15$).
* **RHII:** Relative Heat Island Intensity.
* **BAH, TAH, IAH, MAH:** Anthropogenic heat proxies representing structural urban metabolism.

## 3. Physics & Mathematical Formulas

### 3.1 Liang's Narrowband to Broadband Albedo
Sentinel-2 only captures specific wavelengths. We use Liang's (2001) empirical 5-band formula to estimate total shortwave Albedo:
$$ \alpha = 0.356\rho_{blue} + 0.130\rho_{red} + 0.373\rho_{nir} + 0.085\rho_{swir1} + 0.072\rho_{swir2} - 0.0018 $$

### 3.2 Relative Heat Island Intensity (RHII)
To identify how much hotter the city is compared to its natural state, we find the mean LST of "Cropland" pixels (where NDVI > 0.4 and Albedo < 0.25). 
$$ RHII_i = LST_i - \overline{LST}_{cropland} $$

### 3.3 AHE Empirical Proxies
When structural OSM data times out, AHE is mapped using inverse physical logic (where $NDVI_{norm}$ and $Albedo_{norm}$ are scaled 0 to 1):
* **BAH:** Buildings destroy vegetation. $BAH = (1.0 - NDVI_{norm}) \times 100$
* **TAH:** Asphalt roads lack vegetation *and* have very low albedo. $TAH = (1.0 - NDVI_{norm}) \times (1.0 - Albedo_{norm}) \times 80$

### 3.4 Surface Energy Balance (The PINN Constraint)
In `train_pinn.py`, we implement a simplified Surface Energy Balance. 
1. **Stefan-Boltzmann Law:** The longwave radiation $E$ emitted by the ground requires the absolute temperature in Kelvin:
   $$ T_{Kelvin} = LST_{Celsius} + 273.15 $$
   $$ LW_{out} = \epsilon \sigma (T_{Kelvin})^4 $$
   *(where $\sigma = 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \ W/m^2/K^4$ and $\epsilon \approx 0.97$)*
2. **Available Energy:** The net radiation entering the pixel plus the Anthropogenic heat:
   $$ Available Energy = \left[ (1-\alpha)SW_{in} + LW_{in} - LW_{out} \right] + BAH + TAH $$
3. **The Physics Loss:** On a clear summer day, *Available Energy* must be strictly positive. If the neural network predicts an extremely high hallucinated temperature where $LW_{out}$ eclipses incoming radiation and AHE, it is penalized.

## 4. Code Architecture (Step-by-Step)

The project is structured into four chronologically executed scripts:

### Phase 1: `src/data/gee_extraction.py`
**Purpose:** Connects to Google Earth Engine. 
**Logic:** Defines a bounding box around Delhi-NCR. Computes the median Landsat LST and Sentinel-2 NDVI/Albedo for May 2023. Generates signed URLs and downloads the matrices as raw `.tif` files.

### Phase 2: `src/data/build_features.py`
**Purpose:** Geospatial to Tabular conversion.
**Logic:** Uses `rasterio` to read the `.tif` arrays. Flattens them from 2D grids into 1D arrays and merges them into a Pandas DataFrame. Calculates `LST_Celsius`, filters out clouds (NaNs), identifies the Cropland Baseline, and calculates `RHII`.

### Phase 3: `src/data/build_ahe_proxy.py`
**Purpose:** Feature Injection.
**Logic:** A necessary pivot around OpenStreetMap API timeouts. Reads the tabular data and mathematically calculates BAH, TAH, IAH, and MAH based on the inverse logic of NDVI and Albedo described in section 3.3. Saves the final `delhi_thermal_features.csv`.

### Phase 4: Machine Learning (`src/models/`)
**A. `train_lightgbm.py` (The Baseline)**
*   Trains a highly efficient Gradient Boosting regressor predicting `LST_Celsius` from NDVI, Albedo, BAH, and TAH. 
*   Uses SHAP to prove that high NDVI drives temperatures down, while BAH drives it up.

**B. `train_pinn.py` (The Physics Enforcer)**
*   A Deep PyTorch Neural Network predicting the same target.
*   Contains the custom `physics_informed_loss` function. 
*   Saves the model weights and data scalers to `models/pinn_delhi.pth`.

### Phase 5: `src/models/optimize_scenarios.py`
**Purpose:** Prescriptive Mitigation (Solving the UHI problem).
**Logic:** 
1. Loads the trained PyTorch PINN.
2. Isolates the 100 hottest urban pixels.
3. Defines a `pymoo` Optimization Problem: *Minimize LST by changing NDVI and Albedo.*
4. Enforces a **Constraint**: The total allowed change in NDVI + Albedo across all pixels cannot exceed a 20% aggregate municipal budget.
5. Runs NSGA-II to find the optimal LCZ upgrades and saves the recommended pixel modifications to `data/processed/optimal_scenario.csv`.
